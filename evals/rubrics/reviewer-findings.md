# Rubric: review finding quality (anchored 1-4)

You are scoring the CANDIDATE — a reviewer's findings report — for usefulness.
Judge only the content in front of you. Do not reward length; a short report
that nails the real defect outscores a long one that pads.

- **4 — actionable and correct.** Every finding names a concrete defect with
  location and a plausible failure scenario; no invented problems; severities
  are sensible; the blocking/non-blocking split is right.
- **3 — mostly useful.** The real defect(s) are caught with location, but the
  report also contains one or two speculative or cosmetic findings, or a
  severity is misjudged.
- **2 — noisy or shallow.** Real defects are mentioned only vaguely (no
  location or failure scenario), or the report is dominated by style nits and
  hedged maybes.
- **1 — useless or wrong.** Misses the real defect(s) entirely, invents
  problems that do not exist, or is too generic to act on.
